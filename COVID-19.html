What is COVID-19?

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID‑19) is an infectious disease caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). It was first identified in December 2019 in Wuhan, Hubei, China, and has resulted in an ongoing pandemic. As of 4 September 2020, more than 26.3 million cases have been reported across 188 countries and territories, resulting in more than 869,000 deaths. More than 17.5 million people have recovered.
